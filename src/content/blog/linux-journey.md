# My linux adventure over the years

Recently I started daily driving Linux.  Even though I've been familiar with Linux for many years and have used it for various projects, this is the first time I have consistenly used it as my daily driver for an extended period of time.  And I gotta say, I am addicted and in love.


### My current setup

Before we go on, my current setup:
- Desktop with 2 LG 4k60 HDR10 27" monitors
- AMD Ryzen 7 5800X
- RTX 3070
- 32 GB RAM
- Fedora 39 (latest)
- Hyprland

I have been wanting to try hyprland for longer than I would like to admit, but many problems stood in my way.  But we will get there.


## My first interactions with Linux

I was born in 1989, and as a lot of people from my generation, I grew up watching computers and the internet grow and change the world.  I was always on my PC and online as much as I could, since I can remember.  And somehow, I learned about linux.

Back in the early 2000s I remember I used Live CDs with Ubuntu, mint or Slax (which became my favorite) as a recovery tool for windows.

My grandmother would sometimes bust her pc, often by shutting it down by unplugging the electricity cord, and then it wouldn't turn on anymore. I would use a live CD with slax to backup her files before formatting, or just running some recovery tools, defrag etc, to avoid her losing all her data.

The first time I installed linux was with a friend from school. He was a very smart guy and was interested in programming.  We used to hangout and talk about nerdy stuff and play video games.  We had been talking about Linux for a while and one day he invited me to his house for a crazy adventure and wanted someone to help him: he was going to format his PC and install Linux. And not any linux, he chose to try Slackware, which was going to be a harder installation. M

ind you, at that time, that was the only computer in his house, as would be the case for most households, so that was a big commitment. He did a lot of preparation and I remember he printed (in paper!) the whole installation guide! This was before smartphones, so there was no other way. In the end, it worked out.  We had sooooo much fun.

I don't think I got to trying a real linux installation on my PC until around 2008. I got a new PC and did a  dual-boot Windows + Mint. I wanted to daily drive Mint, but between design school and lots of apps that didn't work, I only used it to try things out.  I remember one day I got home from university to find my father yelling at me.  He had tried  to use my pc to print something, and obviously that didn't work. He said he turned the computer on and it logged into something weird an nothing worked, lol

- last time I tried daily driving linux, was in 2021.  I wanted to run Manjaro. I had just bought my current machine, equipped with 2 4K monitors and a RTX3070. I tried a lot, spent hours in stack overflow, and gave up having manjaro work with my nvidia and monitors setup.

- then I could talk a LOT about my last experience, now in 2026, and having AI help me (instead of 329048390248 stack overflow pages)
