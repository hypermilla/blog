# My linux adventure

I have some old stories about linux:

- back in the early 2000s I used Live CDs with Ubuntu, mint or Slax (which became my favorite) as a recovery tool for windows. My grandmother would sometimes bust her pc, often by shutting it down by unplugging the electricity cord, and then it wouldnt turn on. I would use slax to backup her files before formatting, or just running some recovery tools, defrag etc, to avoid her losing all her data.

- first time I installed linux was with a friend from school. he invited me to his house just to do that. he had printed (in paper) the whole installation guide! it was sooo much fun. This was before phones.

- around 2008 or so I had double boot windows + mint on my pc. I remember my father yelling at me because he wanted to use my pc to print something, he had some issue with his pc, and he couldnt use mine. he said he turned the computer on and it logged into something weird an nothing worked (that was mint lol)

- last time I tried daily driving linux, was in 2021.  I wanted to run Manjaro. I had just bought my current machine, equipped with 2 4K monitors and a RTX3070. I tried a lot, spent hours in stack overflow, and gave up having manjaro work with my nvidia and monitors setup.

- then I could talk a LOT about my last experience, now in 2026, and having AI help me (instead of 329048390248 stack overflow pages)
